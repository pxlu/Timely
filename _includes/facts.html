The Facts

Vulnerable Demographic

Increasingly, university-aged individuals are a growing part of the demographic that is particularly vulnerable to mental health issues, with almost a third of undergraduates reporting symptoms of elevated distress.

Drastic Consequence

In large part due to mental illness, suicide is the 2nd leading cause of death among 15-24 year olds, accounting for 24% of all deaths in this age group. Over 80% of post-secondary students who commit suicide had never participated in counselling services on campus.

Economic Cost

The direct economic burden of mental illness in Canada is over 51 billion dollars in 2015. This figure does not account for indirect factors, such as productivity costs, loss of employment, and others, estimated to encompass over 200 billion when included.
